RURAL DEVELOPMENT

  Rural development is the process of improving the quality of life and economic well-being of people living in rural areas, often relatively isolated and sparsely populated areas.  




Our rural development programs are based on holistic development approach which focuses on providing livelihood opportunities, developing infrastructure, promoting education, improving health care facilities reaching out to every individual in the community, and facilitate getting food and nutrition security for inclusive rural development.

As a part of integrated village development, we are working extensively in the following areas-

•Capacity Building of farmers
By training farmers in new farming technologies, organic farming & crop selection practices we have significantly impacted the individual earnings of families by increasing their productivity by 30-40%.

•Infrastructure Development
Construction of community hall & toilets, refurbishing of aganwadi and Public health centers, installation of community water system and street solar lights, women study center, developing rainwater conservation system, vermicomposting as a part of infrastructure development.

•Health Camps
Organizing general & specialized heath camps and awareness sessions as per community requirement

•Skill Training
A major USP of our projects is the training that we provide to the youth in various skill trades with the aim of improving and increasing their livelihood opportunities and we ensure guaranteed placements in the trade taught to the youth. This is a result of an extensive feasibility study, market analysis, mobilization, training and certification method provided to the youth.

•Self Help Group
Formation of Self -Help groups to encourage collective efforts, self- employment, financial independence.

Renaissance Education Society(R.E.S.) is a non-profit organisation registered under the society act XXI, 1860, was set up in Delhi in 2005. We are comitted towards shaping a positive change and working as catalust for bringing social and exonomic change for underprivileged communities.
